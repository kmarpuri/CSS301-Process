## Purpose

This Graduation Learning Plan shows the best practices to make you a promising candidate for a software engineering role after graduation. It covers building a portfolio of projects, preparing for unique interview questions, and finding ways to land an internship that leads to a full time position. Doing all of these helps you achieve skills employers are seeking.

---

## Building Your Project Portfolio

Projects show real-life applications of subjects learned in class. UWB Career Services reported that recruiters spend on average 6 seconds looking at a resume, showing why your projects and experiences must stand out from the rest of the crowd[1]. Projects also help you find talking points about your strengths during interviews, giving examples of problem solving and technical expertise in the field.

Build at least 3 projects that show proficiency for different skills. Create a full-stack web project that uses a framework such as React.js. Build a data-based project that uses APIs such as Spotify's API, to gather and analyze existing data. Develop a project that uses a lower-level language, such as C++ to make an application that uses data structures. Host all projects on GitHub so recruiters can view your code. Submit around 3 pull-requests on open-source repositories showing proficiency.

## Preparing for Technical Interviews

Software engineering interviews are significantly different from other fields as they require months of prep before even attempting an interview, compared to normally studying for an exam. While what you learn in college lets you take your time to solve questions, these interviews test how well you perform under pressure. Companies test you with a live coding exercise, system design questions and how you work with others.

In your junior to senior year, dedicate around 5-10 hours a week to prepping for interviews. Study data structures and algorithms through LeetCode style questions with websites like NeetCode[2]. Read about system design using around 5-10 engineering blogs from companies such as Netflix. Practice mock interviews on websites such as Pramp to make sure you're ready for interviews[3].

## Securing Internship Experience

Companies use internships to gauge potential full-time employees at their company. This makes internships prime opportunities to secure a job before even graduating, or to convince other companies that you know how to work in a job environment.

Finish internship applications in the summer before the internship. To increase your chances of getting selected for a second round, apply to at least 50 internships. Use the SimplifyJobs GitHub repo for Summer Internships[4]. Attend around 10 hackathons and tech meetings to meet experts in the field who can refer you for an internship.
